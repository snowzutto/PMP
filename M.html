What are inputs and outputs and how do they relate to process groups in the PMBOK?

The Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK) defines five process groups, each consisting of a set of related processes that are necessary to complete a project. Inputs are the information and materials that are required to start a process, while outputs are the results of a process. In many cases, the outputs of one process become the inputs of another process. This allows for a seamless transition between process groups and ensures that the project is completed successfully.

For example, the inputs to the planning process group might include the project charter, stakeholder requirements, and historical project data. The outputs of the planning process group might include the project management plan, project documents, and the project schedule. These outputs are the result of planning the project and are used as inputs to the execution process group.

Understanding the inputs and outputs of each process group is essential to successfully managing a project according to the PMBOK. By ensuring that the necessary inputs are provided and that the outputs are produced as expected, a project manager can ensure that the project is completed on time, on budget, and to the required level of quality.
